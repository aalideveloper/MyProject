Concave Lense Glasess


Concave lenses have at least one surface curved inside.

A concave lens is also known as a diverging lens.

It is shaped round inwards at the centre and bulges outwards through the edges, making the light diverge.

They are used to treat myopia as they make faraway objects look smaller than they are.

Convex Lense Glasess

A convex lens is a type of lens that is thicker in the middle than at the edges.

This shape causes light rays to come together or converge when they pass through it.

Because of this, convex lenses are also known as converging lenses.

They are commonly used in magnifying glasses and to correct farsightedness (hyperopia) by making nearby objects appear clearer.
