A concave lens is a type of lens that is thinner in the middle than at the edges.

This shape causes light rays to spread out or diverge when they pass through it.

concave lenses are also known as diverging lenses12.

They are commonly used in glasses to correct nearsightedness (myopia) by making distant objects appear clearer


A convex lens is a type of lens that is thicker in the middle than at the edges.
This shape causes light rays to come together or converge when they pass through it.

Because of this, convex lenses are also known as converging lenses.
